Roast Potatoes

The true stars of any roast dinner, roast potatoes hold a special place in my heart. They are the ultimate comfort food: no roast is complete without them.

My recipe takes spuds back to their basics. Nothing fancy: no special fat; no overbaring seasonings; no intricate tecniques. Just spuds, fat, salt, and pepper. Perfection.

Ingredients

  • Potatoes. Ideally Marris Pipers or Yukon Golds
  • Oil. Has to have a high smoke point: olive oil is a no go. Vegetable, rapeseed (canola for the Americans), or sunflower are good here.
  • Salt and pepper.

Instructions

  1. Peel the potatoes and cut them into roughly even sized pieces. An average size potato should be cut into roughly 4, but the main thing is keeping them even.
  2. Add your peeled, chopped potatoes to a saucepan of cold, salted water. Bring this pan to a boil.
  3. While your potatoes are boiling, add a good glug of oil to a baking disk or roasting tray. You want something that holds heat well, and has high sides. Your oil should completely coat the bottom of the pan. Pop this in a 220° oven to heat up.
  4. Check on your boiling spuds. They should be soft, but still hold their shapes. The edges should be starting to crumble.
  5. Drain your spuds in a colander. Cover with paper towel or a tea towel and leave to steam dry for at least 10 minutes.
  6. Give your spuds a hefty shake. You want to break up the edges here: the more edges; the more crispy bits.
  7. Remove your hot, oiled pan from the oven and add the potatoes. Give them a sprinkle of salt, and toss in the oil with tongs to get everyone coated.
  8. Return the pan to the oven for 20 minutes, then take them out and flip then. Lower the head to 180° and cook until golden and crispy all round. Arpox 30-40 minutes, but keep an eye on them
  9. Once your potatoes are beautiful and golden, remoove them from the oven. Season with a generous sprinkle of pepper, and add more salt to taste. Let cool for a 3-5 mins, and enjoy hot.
